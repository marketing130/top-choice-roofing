What We Offer

Flat & Low-Slope Roofing Installation

Flat and low-slope roofing requires a different approach than pitched roofing — proper drainage design, seam integrity, and membrane selection are critical to long-term performance. At Top Choice Roofing and Siding, we install three proven flat roofing systems, each suited to specific performance needs and budgets.

TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin) is our most popular flat roofing membrane — highly reflective, energy-efficient, and heat-welded at the seams for a watertight bond. EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer) is a durable rubber membrane ideal for residential applications with excellent UV and ozone resistance. Modified Bitumen is a multi-layer asphalt-based system offering exceptional puncture resistance for high-traffic roof areas.

Every installation includes a full drainage assessment, proper insulation, and perimeter flashing sealed to manufacturer specifications — ensuring your flat roof performs reliably through freeze-thaw cycles and heavy snowfall.

Flat Roofing FAQs

With proper installation and maintenance, TPO and EPDM flat roofs typically last 15–25 years. Modified bitumen systems average 15–20 years. Annual inspections and prompt repair of any membrane punctures or seam issues are the most important factors in maximizing lifespan.
The most common causes of flat roof failure are ponding water from inadequate drainage, seam separation, membrane punctures, and improper flashing at penetrations (vents, skylights, HVAC units). All of these are addressable through quality installation and routine inspection.
It depends on your building and priorities. TPO is our top recommendation for most applications — it's energy-efficient, durable, and offers the best value. EPDM is ideal for smaller residential applications. Modified bitumen suits high-traffic or heavily penetrated roofs. We'll recommend the best option after inspecting your property.
Yes — flat roofs are engineered to handle the snow loads typical for Ontario. The critical factors are proper structural design and ensuring the roof is kept clear of excessive accumulation. We assess your structure as part of every flat roofing project to ensure it meets current load requirements.
Signs that your flat roof may need replacement include persistent leaks after repairs, visible membrane blistering or cracking, ponding water that doesn't drain within 48 hours, and a roof age exceeding 20 years. Contact us for a free inspection — we'll give you an honest assessment.
