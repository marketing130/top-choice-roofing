What We Offer

Standing Seam & Metal Panel Roofing for Commercial Properties

Durable, low-maintenance commercial metal roofing including standing seam and exposed fastener R-panel systems. Built to last 40–60 years with minimal upkeep.

Commercial metal roofing is one of the most cost-effective long-term investments a property owner can make. With virtually no ongoing maintenance requirements and a lifespan that far exceeds conventional roofing materials, metal roofing delivers exceptional return on investment for industrial, retail, and institutional buildings.

Our experienced installation crews handle everything from small commercial re-roofs to large-scale industrial projects. We work with premium steel and aluminum panel systems, ensuring proper underlayment, ventilation, and flashing details that meet or exceed Ontario Building Code requirements.

Common Questions

Commercial Metal Roofing FAQs

Common questions about metal roofing for commercial and industrial properties.

Standing seam metal roofing uses concealed fasteners with raised seams that interlock — no exposed screws means no penetrations through the metal and a virtually watertight roof. R-panel (exposed fastener) systems are installed faster and cost less, with screws driven through the panel face. Both are highly durable; standing seam is preferred for long-term performance and aesthetics, while R-panel is common on industrial and agricultural buildings where cost efficiency is the priority.
Quality commercial metal roofing systems typically last 40–60 years. Standing seam systems with Kynar-coated steel or aluminum panels can last even longer when properly maintained. Compare this to 15–25 years for asphalt shingles or 20–30 years for flat membrane systems. The higher upfront cost of metal roofing is offset by dramatically lower lifecycle costs.
On commercial buildings with proper insulation and underlayment, metal roofing is not significantly louder than other roofing materials. The combination of rigid insulation, a structural deck, and interior ceiling assemblies absorbs sound effectively. In industrial or open-structure buildings without ceilings, some additional noise is normal — but most business owners report that it is not a concern.
In many cases, yes — metal roofing can be installed over existing shingles using a batten system, which eliminates tear-off costs and adds an additional layer of insulation. However, this depends on the condition of the existing roof, the structural load capacity of the building, and local building code requirements. We'll assess your existing roof and advise on the best approach for your project.
