What We Offer

Quality Engineered Wood Siding Installation & Replacement

Engineered wood siding combines the authentic texture and warmth of real wood with the durability and low-maintenance performance that modern homeowners demand. Made from wood fibres bonded with resins and treated for moisture and pest resistance, it outperforms natural wood in almost every category.

At Top Choice Roofing and Siding, we install engineered wood siding products from trusted manufacturers including LP SmartSide — one of the most durable and beautiful siding systems available for Canadian homes. Available in lap, panel, and shingle profiles to suit any architectural style.

Every installation includes proper moisture barrier preparation, precision trimming, and factory-finish or field-painted options to achieve the exact look you want. Our craftsmen ensure tight, weather-resistant joints that keep your home protected for decades.

Why Choose Engineered Wood Siding

The Best of Both Worlds

Engineered wood siding offers the natural look of wood with none of the maintenance headaches — here's why GTA homeowners love it.

🌳

Natural Look

Engineered wood is virtually indistinguishable from real wood — deep grain textures and warm tones deliver authentic curb appeal at a fraction of the cost.

💪

Impact Resistant

Engineered composite construction makes it far more resistant to dents, dings, and storm damage than standard wood or vinyl alternatives.

🌟

30+ Year Lifespan

Quality engineered wood siding is built to last 30 years or more with only routine cleaning — a long-term investment in your home's value and protection.

✅

Low Maintenance vs Real Wood

Unlike natural wood, engineered wood won't rot, split, or require frequent repainting. Repainting cycles are significantly longer — often 15+ years between full repaints.

🌿

Eco-Friendly

Made from sustainably sourced wood fibre, engineered wood siding uses less raw timber than solid wood products while delivering superior performance.

🏠

Versatile Styles

Available in lap siding, vertical panels, shakes, and trim — giving you a complete exterior system that looks cohesive from every angle.

Common Questions

Engineered Wood Siding FAQs

Answers to the questions we hear most often about engineered wood siding.

Engineered wood siding is made from wood fibres and strands bonded with adhesive resins and treated with a zinc-borate preservative for rot and pest resistance. It looks virtually identical to real wood but is far more dimensionally stable — it won't shrink, swell, or split the way solid wood does under Ontario's freeze-thaw cycles.
Most engineered wood siding comes with a factory-applied primer and can be painted any colour on site, or ordered pre-painted from the manufacturer. Unlike solid wood, you won't need to repaint as frequently — typical repainting intervals are 10–15 years rather than every 5–7 years with natural wood.
Yes — engineered wood siding is an excellent choice for Ontario's climate. The composite construction handles freeze-thaw cycles better than solid wood because it expands and contracts less. Products like LP SmartSide are specifically tested and warranted for performance in cold, wet climates.
Quality engineered wood siding typically lasts 30 years or more with proper installation and routine maintenance (keeping it painted and caulked at trim joints). Many manufacturers offer 50-year limited warranties on their products, giving you confidence in the long-term investment.

Hardie board (fibre cement) is heavier, fire-resistant, and pest-proof, while engineered wood is lighter, easier to work with, and offers more realistic wood grain textures at a typically lower installed cost.
