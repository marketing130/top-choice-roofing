[Your siding](https://test.topchoiceroofingandsiding.com/siding.html) is one of the most important parts of your home’s exterior. It helps protect your house from harsh weather, improves curb appeal, and plays a role in keeping your home comfortable throughout the year. But like any exterior material, siding does not last forever.

One of the most common signs that your siding may need replacing is visible damage. Cracks, warping, loose panels, or holes can allow moisture to get behind the siding and into the structure of your home. Once water gets in, it can lead to mould, rot, and more expensive repairs.

Fading is another sign to watch for. While some colour change is normal over time, severely faded siding may mean the material has weakened and is no longer providing the same level of protection. If your siding looks worn out no matter how often you clean it, an upgrade may be worth considering.

You may also notice higher energy bills or drafts inside your home. Damaged or poorly installed siding can affect your home’s insulation, making it harder to maintain a comfortable indoor temperature during hot summers and cold Canadian winters.

Replacing your siding can give your home a fresh new look while helping protect it from the elements. If your siding is showing signs of age or damage, scheduling an inspection is a smart first step.
