Your roof is one of the most important parts of your home. It protects your property from rain, snow, wind, heat, and everything in between. But like anything else, roofing materials wear down over time. Knowing when to replace your roof can help you avoid costly repairs, water damage, and bigger problems down the road.

One of the first signs to watch for is missing, cracked, or curling shingles. If your shingles are no longer sitting flat or you notice pieces coming loose after a storm, your roof may be reaching the end of its lifespan. Dark streaks, moss growth, and granule loss can also be signs that your shingles are deteriorating.

Leaks are another major warning sign. Even a small water stain on your ceiling can point to a bigger issue above. Roof leaks are not always obvious right away, which is why it is important to have your roof inspected if you notice moisture, mould, or unexplained damage inside your home.

Age also plays a big role. Many asphalt shingle roofs last around 15 to 25 years, depending on the quality of materials, installation, ventilation, and weather exposure. If your roof is getting older and showing visible wear, it may be more cost-effective to replace it instead of continuing to repair it.
