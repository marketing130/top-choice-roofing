What We Offer

Commercial-Grade Asphalt Shingles for Sloped Roofs

Commercial-grade asphalt shingles for sloped commercial roofs — office buildings, retail plazas, and multi-unit residential. Durable, cost-effective, and available in a range of profiles and colours.

Commercial shingle roofing combines the proven reliability of asphalt with heavy-duty construction suited to the demands of commercial use. Our commercial shingle installations use higher-weight shingles with enhanced wind and impact resistance ratings, designed to protect your building for decades.

We work with leading shingle manufacturers to offer the right product for every commercial application — from architectural laminates for office buildings and retail plazas to high-performance impact-resistant shingles for properties in hail-prone areas. Every installation is backed by both a manufacturer's product warranty and our workmanship guarantee.

Common Questions

Commercial Shingle Roofing FAQs

Common questions about commercial-grade asphalt shingles for sloped roofs.

Commercial-grade shingles are typically heavier, carry higher wind resistance ratings, and are available in impact-resistant (Class 4) versions suited to the larger roof areas and greater exposure of commercial buildings. They are installed to meet commercial building code standards and are available with longer warranty terms through manufacturer programs designed for commercial applications.
Commercial architectural shingles typically last 25–35 years depending on the product specification, climate exposure, and ventilation quality. Impact-resistant and premium laminate shingles can extend that range further. Regular inspections and minor maintenance (resealing exposed fasteners, replacing isolated damaged shingles) help maximize the lifespan of any shingle roof.
Asphalt shingles are designed for sloped roofs — typically 4:12 pitch or steeper for standard installation, or 2:12 and above with enhanced underlayment specifications. They are not suitable for flat or very low slope roofs (below 2:12), which require a membrane system such as TPO, EPDM, or modified bitumen. We'll assess your roof's pitch and recommend the right system for your building.
