What We Offer

Cedar Shake Roofing Installation & Replacement

Cedar shake roofing delivers a rich, natural look that no synthetic material can truly replicate. The rough-split texture and warm tones of western red cedar create a distinctive aesthetic that adds warmth and character to any home — from heritage properties to contemporary builds.

Cedar is naturally resistant to insects and decay, and its cellular structure provides thermal insulation superior to most other roofing materials. A well-maintained cedar shake roof lasts 25–30 years and improves with age as it weathers to a beautiful silver-grey patina.

At Top Choice Roofing and Siding, our cedar shake installations follow best practices for ventilation, spacing, and moisture management. We use pressure-treated breather strips and proper underlayment to extend the life of your shake roof and prevent moisture-related issues before they start.

Common Questions

Cedar Shake Roofing FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about cedar shake roofing.

A properly installed cedar shake roof typically lasts 25–30 years in Ontario's climate. Lifespan is significantly influenced by the quality of installation, ventilation, and periodic maintenance such as moss treatment and inspection after major storms.
Yes — cedar shake roofs benefit from periodic cleaning to remove moss, lichen, and debris, as well as a preservative treatment every 5–7 years to maintain the wood's natural oils and extend lifespan. We offer maintenance packages to keep your cedar roof in top condition.
Untreated cedar has a Class C fire rating. However, pressure-treated fire-retardant cedar shakes are available that achieve a Class A or B rating — the same as asphalt shingles. We discuss all options with you upfront to ensure your installation meets local building code requirements.
Cedar shakes are split from the wood block, giving them a rough, textured face. Cedar shingles are sawn smooth on both sides and have a more refined appearance. Shakes are thicker and more rustic; shingles are more uniform and tapered. We install both — the right choice depends on your home's style and your aesthetic preferences.
Absolutely. Many homeowners choose to replace aging cedar shake roofs with synthetic slate or high-end asphalt shingles that mimic the wood look with lower maintenance requirements. We can walk you through all your options and help you find the right material for your home and budget.
