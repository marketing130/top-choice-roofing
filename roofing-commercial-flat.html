What We Offer

Flat Roofing Systems for Commercial Properties

TPO, EPDM, and modified bitumen systems built for durability, weather resistance, and long-term performance on low-slope and flat commercial roofs.

Our certified roofing teams have installed and maintained flat roofing systems on commercial buildings of all sizes — from small retail units to large industrial facilities. We work with the leading membrane manufacturers to ensure your flat roof meets the highest performance standards.

Every installation begins with a thorough assessment of your building's drainage, insulation, and substrate condition. We design each system to maximize lifespan, minimize maintenance costs, and protect your investment for decades to come.

Common Questions

Commercial Flat Roofing FAQs

Common questions about flat roofing systems for commercial properties.

The best system depends on your building's use, climate exposure, budget, and existing rooftop equipment. TPO is popular for its energy efficiency and weldable seams. EPDM is a proven performer in cold climates. Modified bitumen offers excellent durability in high-traffic situations. We'll assess your building and recommend the system that provides the best long-term value.
With proper installation and regular maintenance, TPO and EPDM systems typically last 20–30 years. Modified bitumen systems can last 15–20 years. Factors like UV exposure, rooftop traffic, drainage quality, and maintenance frequency all affect lifespan. A preventive maintenance program can significantly extend the life of any membrane system.
In some cases, yes — a recover (overlay) installation can save on tear-off costs. However, this depends on the condition of the existing substrate, the number of existing membrane layers, and local building code requirements. We'll inspect your roof and advise whether a full tear-off or a recover installation is the right approach for your situation.
Ponding water is the primary enemy of flat roofs. We address drainage through proper slope engineering (tapered insulation), correctly sized and positioned interior drains, and secondary overflow drains. During installation we verify that water flows toward drains rather than pooling. If your existing roof has drainage issues, we can redesign the drainage system as part of a re-roofing project.
