What We Offer

Professional Soffit & Fascia Installation & Replacement

Soffit and fascia are two of the most important — and most overlooked — components of your home's exterior. The fascia is the vertical board that runs along the roofline, protecting the roof edge and supporting your eavestroughs. The soffit is the horizontal surface beneath the eave overhang, responsible for ventilating your attic and keeping pests out.

At Top Choice Roofing and Siding, we install aluminum and vinyl soffit and fascia systems that are durable, low-maintenance, and colour-matched to complement your siding and roofing. Whether you're replacing rotted wood fascia, upgrading to ventilated aluminum soffit, or completing a full exterior renovation, we handle it all.

Proper soffit and fascia installation protects your home from moisture infiltration, improves attic ventilation to extend roof life, and gives your exterior a clean, finished appearance. We work cleanly and efficiently, often completing soffit and fascia replacement in a single day.

Why Replace Your Soffit & Fascia

Protection You Can't Afford to Ignore

Damaged or inadequate soffit and fascia causes serious problems over time — here's what quality installation protects against.

💧

Prevents Moisture Damage

Properly installed fascia seals the roof edge from rain and snowmelt, stopping water from penetrating behind your siding and rotting your roof deck or wall framing.

🌬

Improves Ventilation

Vented soffit allows fresh air into your attic, balancing airflow with ridge vents and reducing heat buildup in summer and ice damming in winter.

🐛

Pest Barrier

Gaps in soffit and fascia are a primary entry point for birds, squirrels, bats, and insects. A tight installation closes those entry points and protects your attic.

🏠

Curb Appeal

Fresh, colour-matched soffit and fascia give your home a clean, finished roofline — one of the most noticeable improvements you can make to your home's exterior.

🌁

Protects Roof Edge

Fascia supports the bottom row of shingles and your eavestroughs. When it deteriorates, the entire roof edge system is compromised — affecting drainage and structural integrity.

✅

Low Maintenance

Modern aluminum and vinyl soffit and fascia never need painting, won't rot, and won't attract pests — a significant upgrade over the original wood found on older homes.

Common Questions

Soffit & Fascia FAQs

Answers to the questions we hear most often about soffit and fascia replacement.

The fascia is the vertical board that runs along the edge of the roofline — it faces outward and is where your eavestroughs attach. The soffit is the horizontal surface beneath the eave overhang that faces downward — it's what you see when you look up at your roofline from the ground. Both work together to protect your roof edge and attic.
Common signs include peeling paint, visible rot or soft spots, sagging sections, pest entry points, water staining on exterior walls below the roofline, or eavestroughs that are pulling away from the house. If your fascia board is soft to the touch, it needs to be replaced immediately before moisture damage spreads further into the roof structure.
We primarily install aluminum soffit and fascia — it's durable, maintenance-free, never rots or requires painting, and is available in a wide range of colours. Vinyl soffit is also available. Both are significant upgrades over the original wood soffit and fascia found on many older GTA homes.
Yes — vented soffit is strongly recommended for most homes. It allows outside air to enter the attic and flow up through ridge vents, preventing heat and moisture buildup that causes ice damming in winter and premature shingle degradation in summer. We install fully vented aluminum soffit on nearly all residential projects.
Yes — soffit and fascia replacement is typically a standalone exterior project that does not require touching your roofing. We often complete it at the same time as siding or eavestrough work for efficiency, but it can be done entirely independently of your roof on any home in good structural condition.
